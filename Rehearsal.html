THE REHEARSAL

Characters

Miko: The directorl
Patsy: Assitant director
Julian: Actor
Ruth: Actor
Maggie: Actor
Celia: Actor

The scene is a BBC recording studio. The cast are beginning to assemble in the studio for a rehearsal of a radio play to be broadcast later.

Miko: Good afternoon everyone, nice to see you all here

(There is a chorus of Hello's, hi etc)

Ruth: Brr it's very cold in here

Patsy: It'll soon warm up Ruth

Julian: I thought that when I got a job at the BBC it would at least be warm.

Miko: As Patsy said, it'll soon warm up. Now lets get on. Do you all have your scripts?

(There is a chorus of yes)

Miko: Good! So we can make a start.

Patsy: Are you all happy with the parts you are playing?

Maggie: To be honest I am not really happy about my part.

Miko: Why not Maggie? It is really a good part

Patsy: If I was an actor I'd love that part.

Maggie: It is not a bad part but it says here that I have to kiss Lord Brummel, which is played by Julian

Miko: Yes, that's right, so what's the problem

Maggie: The problem is, I am just about to get married – on Saturday actually

Patsy: The kiss doesn't have to be that long

Celia: I don't think even I could manage a four day kiss

Julian: I might like to give it a try though.

Maggie: You know that is not what I meant. I am not sure I want to be kissing someone in a play four days before the wedding?

Julian: Is there an acceptable number of days when it is ok then?

Miko: (A little exasperated) This is radio Maggie.

Maggie: I know but even so I don't want my future husband hearing me kiss someone just before the wedding.

Ruth: You don't have to kiss him silly

Maggie: But it says I do in the script

Ruth: But as Miko said, this is radio, you don't actually have to kiss him, just sound like you do

Maggie: But is that really acting?

Miko: For goodness sake Maggie it's not that difficult – we'll try to sort it out later. So, is everyone else happy?

Julian: Actually I wouldn't feel very comfortable doing that.

Miko: Doing what Julian.

Julian:You know, that pretending to kiss malarkey. I think if there is a kiss in the script then it should be a proper kiss, not a pretend one.

Patsy: As Miko keeps pointing out Julian, this is radio, does it really matter?

Julian: Yes it does! I am an actor and a damn good one. I can't be doing with kissing the back of my own hand for a sound effect.

Ruth: You are being a bit of a prima donna Julian

Julian: I am certainly not, I just want some realism in the production

Celia: On page 42 they get onto a train – do you want Miko to bring one into the studio?

Julian: That is ridiculous and you know it

Celia: No worse than what you are saying

Miko: Please, please can we just start the rehearsal? We can sort the kissing business out when we get to it.

Patsy: Yes, lets do that and make a start, Scripts open everybody and Celia, you have the first line

Celia: I can't believe that on such a beautiful morning my heart is so heavy

Ruth: Eleven o clock

Miko. Whoa, whoa, just a minute what do you mean eleven o clock? That's not in the script

Ruth: Yes it is

Celia: Well it's not in mine

Julian: Or mine

Ruth: Oh dear, I am so sorry everyone, I have brought the wrong script. This is for a tv ad I am doing next week for a chocolate bar

Patsy: Do you have the right script with you?

Ruth: No, I am afraid not

Patsy: Never mind I will get you another one. Here you are

Ruth: Thank you

Patsy: You are welcome

Ruth: So what page are we on?

Miko: ( still exasperated) We are still on the first line of page one!

Ruth: Ah yes, here we are

Patsy: Can we have your line again please Celia?

Celia: Yes, of course

Patsy: Thank you

Celia: My heart is so heavy on this beautiful morning....

Miko: Whoa, whoa, just a minute. That is not what is says in the script

Celia: I know but I thought it sounded better

Julien: I didn't.

Patsy: Nor did I. Please stick to the script Celia. Now, once more please

Celia: Oh alright. (short pause)I can't believe that on such a beautiful morning my heart is so heavy

Ruth:My dear you must not concern yourself so much over that awful Lord Brummel

(There is quite a long pause)

Patsy: Maggie, this is your line, wakey wakey

Maggie: Oh yes I am the maid aren't I

Miko: Yes you are

Maggie: ok. (clears her throat) Here is the tea Madam oh

Patsy: That's not right Maggie, you don't just say oh. You are surprised. You have just seen Lord Brummel about to creep through the French windows. It is a very loud OH! And you drop the tea tray.

Maggie: But I haven't got a tea tray?

Miko: You don't need one, the sound effects team will do that

Julien: I don't really want to creep through the window

Miko: Why ever not?

Julien: I just don't see him as a creepy sort of guy, more of a bursting into the room sort of chap

Patsy: Well the author sees him as a creepy sort of guy so that's how we'll do it

Julien: Maybe the author got it wrong?

Miko: How could he have got it wrong? He wrote it.

Maggie: And I don't like the idea of someone else dropping the tray. I think I should do that – it would be more realistic

Celia: Perhaps we should get a train into the studio then

Miko: Let me get this clear - There will be no airplane in the studio, sound effects will drop the tray and Lord Brummel will creep though the French windows. Is that understood by everyone?

Julien: There is one further point I would like to make

Miko: And what is that Julien?

Julien: How do we know they are French windows?

Patsy: What do you mean

Julien: Well as everyone keeps reminding me, this is radio. How will the people know if I am coming through French windows or sliding down the chimney

Miko: Have you actually read the script Julien?

Julien: Errr no, not as yet.

Patsy: Had you read it you would know that later on it is discussed that you came though the French windows

Julien: Ah, I see. That's alright then

Miko: So can we now get on?

Maggie: Could we have a cup of coffee before continuing?

Miko: NO!!!! we have to get this rehearsal underway and so far we are not off the first page

Patsy: Your line Celia

Celia: Oh my goodness! what is it my dear

Maggie: The window! There is someone at the French Windows Madam

(There is a pause)

Patsy: (With a big sigh) Julien it is your line

Julien: ehh, what? Oh yes, yes. Sorry. I was reading the script

Miko: Julien, the time to read the script is when it drops onto your doormat, Not when we are actually rehearsing the play. So your line please.

Julien: Yes of course. Ahem. - Aha so you think Lord Brummel is awful do you my dear

Ruth: Yes, indeed I do, the most awful man I have ever met

Julien: Maybe even more awful than you can imagine. Now who is this pretty little thing over here

Celia: She is my maid, keep away from her.

Julien: Not a very good maid if she drops the tea tray so easily

Maggie: You scared me.

Julien: My apologies my dear. Mmm what perfectly kissable you lips you have

Maggie: Stay right there,get away from me

Miko: Whoa, whoa, that is not in the script.

Maggie: l know it is not in the script but Julien was about to kiss me

Julien: I was most certainly not

Celia: You were moving towards her

Julien: To get the feel of my part

Miko: Can we please stop this bickering and get on with the rehearsal?

Patsy: Maggie it's your line

Maggie: Does anyone mind if I make a quick call first?

Miko: A QUICK CALL!!!! Maggie this is a rehearsal. It's not the time to make phone calls

Maggie: It would only be a quick one

Celia: I do agree with Miko, we should get on

Patsy: It will be lunchtime soon and we haven't got past the first page yet.

Ruth: Speaking of lunch, I am already quite peckish.

Julien. I haven't even had coffee yet

Miko: Let's make a deal, Can we at least get through the first act, then we can have coffee, make phone calls and eat lunch and I will feel then that at least we have got some work done

Maggie: Ok, I'll make the call later. It was only to my future husband to remind him how much I love him

Ruth: Ohhh Yuck!!!

Maggie: Shut up Ruth, maybe you do not know what it is like to be truly in love.

Ruth: I am glad I don't if it would make me like that.

Patsy: Please, please ladies, can we get on?

Maggie:Oh ok.

Patsy: Julian, can we take it from your last line please.

Julien: Of course – Mmmm. What perfectly kissable lips you have

Maggie: Oh Lord Brummel, you make me blush so

Ruth: Brummel you are nothing but a lecherous beast, leave the poor girl alone.

Celia: You should leave right now, your company is not wanted here.

Julien: But she is so adorable I cannot stop myself from kissing her

Maggie: Oh Lord Brummel, no please

Miko: What happened there? There should have been the sound of a kiss

Julien: I kissed the back of my hand

Patsy: Well it sounded nothing like a kiss

Ruth: I agree

Celia: So do I

Miko: Can we please make it sound more lifelike?

Julien: I did say that it wouldn't work

Maggie: Well he is not going to kiss me

Celia: I have an idea

Patsy: What is it ?

Celia: Why don't Maggie and I swap parts.

Patsy: Maybe we could do that.

Ruth: I don't mind swapping either. I have done enough kissing on film to not let it bother me.

Miko: I really think it is too late in the day to start swapping roles.

Patsy: I agree but we are going nowhere like this

Miko: Is there really no way we can make this more convincing.

Julien: I am an actor and if I have to act the part I need to have some realism to it.

Patsy: How would you feel about swapping parts Maggie?

Maggie: I really don't see why I should swap parts just because HE wants to do it His way

Julian: I just think it is the right way to make it sound realistic.

Patsy: We seemed to have reached a stale mate.

Celia: Please Maggie do try it? It is just one kiss you are not going to be rolling about on the bed naked.

Ruth: You are supposed to be an actress Maggie. Actresses kiss all the time on TV shows and movies.

Patsy: And it doesn't seem to matter to them if they are married, engaged or anything else

Maggie: Hmm I suppose you are right, maybe I am being a little too sensitive?

Miko: Hurrah! Does that mean you will do it then?

Maggie: Yes I suppose so

Patsy: Thank goodness for that, we can get on

Celia: At last we are seeing some sense

Patsy: Ok everybody, so can we please take it from Maggie's last line?

Maggie: ok - Oh Lord Brummel, no please....

( there is the sound of long intense kissing)

Celia: For someone that didn't want to do it she is certainly going at it now

Ruth: Maybe we are going for the four day one after all

Patsy: This is far better than I expected

Miko: This does show that after all she can act really well

Ruth: Are they ever going to stop?

Celia: We will miss lunch at the rate

Patsy: Hello you too I think that is a long enough kiss.

Maggie ( A long satisfied sigh, in a soft dreamy voice) I absolutely must make that call now – The wedding is off!

THE END
